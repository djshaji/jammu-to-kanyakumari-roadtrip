## Goa to Mumbai (Day 14: Jan 7)
- **Start Location:** Goa
- **End Location:** Mumbai, MH
- **Approx. Distance:** ≈590 km
- **Key Stops/Notes:** Scenic coastal and Western Ghats drive along NH66. Plan for 10–12 hours with stops.

---
### Fastest Route (National Highways)
**Route:**
Goa → Sawantwadi (NH66) → Ratnagiri (NH66) → Chiplun (NH66) → Panvel (NH66) → Mumbai (NH66)

**Major Highway:** NH66 (entire stretch)

**Toll Plazas (approximate):**
- Goa exit toll (NH66)
- Sawantwadi Toll Plaza (NH66)
- Ratnagiri Toll Plaza (NH66)
- Chiplun Toll Plaza (NH66)
- Panvel Toll Plaza (NH66)
- Mumbai entry toll (NH66)

**Notes:**
This route is the fastest and most reliable, with good road conditions and facilities. Expect tolls at major highway sections. FASTag recommended.

---
### Tourist Attractions & Landmarks (En-route/Minor Detour)

1. **Fort Aguada** (Goa) – Historic fort. (0 km detour)
2. **Calangute & Baga Beaches** (Goa) – Popular beaches. (0 km detour)
3. **Sawantwadi Palace** (Sawantwadi) – Historic palace. (~3 km detour from NH66)
4. **Sindhudurg Fort** (near Malvan) – Iconic sea fort. (~20 km detour from NH66)
5. **Ganpatipule Beach & Temple** (near Ratnagiri) – Famous beach and temple. (~30 km detour from NH66)
6. **Ratnadurga Fort** (Ratnagiri) – Historic fort. (~5 km detour from NH66)
7. **Parshuram Temple** (near Chiplun) – Ancient temple. (~8 km detour from NH66)
8. **Karnala Bird Sanctuary & Fort** (near Panvel) – Nature reserve and fort. (~12 km detour from NH66)
9. **Gateway of India** (Mumbai) – Iconic monument. (0 km detour)
10. **Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus** (Mumbai) – UNESCO World Heritage railway station. (0 km detour)
11. **Marine Drive & Chowpatty Beach** (Mumbai) – Famous promenade and beach. (0 km detour)
12. **Elephanta Caves** (Mumbai) – UNESCO World Heritage site. (~11 km detour from Gateway of India by ferry)
